Kelanese has been observed to function as the lingua franca of the island nation of Kelan. While a number of regional and community-specific speech varieties exist, Kelanese serves as the primary medium of interregional communication.

This document presents a preliminary reference grammar of Kelanese, based on sustained observational fieldwork conducted on the island nation of Kelan. The descriptions herein reflect patterns that have been consistently attested, though they may not account for all regional variation. Given the constraints of external observation and the diversity of speech communities within Kelan, certain analyses remain provisional and subject to change or revision as additional data becomes available.
